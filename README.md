# Functional_analysis_heart_rate

Wearable devices like the Apple Watch generate thousands of heart rate data points every single day. While most basic health apps simply average these numbers out, researchers and data scientists are increasingly turning to Functional Data Analysis (FDA) to get a much more realistic and accurate picture of human behavior.

Moving from Data Points to Continuous Curves
Instead of looking at heart rate as a massive spreadsheet of isolated timestamps, FDA treats the entire 24-hour period as one continuous mathematical curve.

Clustering Different Types of Routine Days
Once the raw data is transformed into smooth daily curves, algorithms (like Functional Expectation Maximization or specialized time-series clustering) can group similar days together based on their mathematical shapes.
